Objective

Provide a sample structure for an AI Use SOP that helps NGOs adopt AI tools safely, ethically, and in line with data protection and responsible AI principles.

Illustrative AI Use Principles

  • Do no harm: AI use must not increase risks for communities, staff, or partners.
  • Privacy and data protection: No personally identifiable or sensitive data may be pasted into public AI tools.
  • Human oversight: AI assists staff; it does not replace professional judgment or accountability.
  • Transparency: Be clear internally about where and how AI is used in workflows and decisions.
  • Equity and inclusion: Regularly check outputs for bias and non-discriminatory language.

Sample SOP Structure

  1. Scope and purpose – who the SOP applies to and why AI is being used.
  2. Approved tools – list of AI tools, access rules, and licensing requirements.
  3. Permitted and prohibited uses – what content can be processed, and what must never be shared.
  4. Data protection and confidentiality – guidance aligned with organizational policies and relevant law.
  5. Review and quality assurance – steps for fact-checking, bias checks, and supervisor approval where needed.
  6. Training and support – minimum induction for staff and focal points for questions.
  7. Monitoring and updates – how the organization will learn from practice and revise the SOP.

Deliverables (Illustrative)

  • Short, practical AI Use SOP tailored to an NGO context.
  • One-page staff-friendly “dos and don’ts” for everyday AI use.
  • Slides for an internal briefing on responsible and safe AI adoption.
