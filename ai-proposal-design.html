Objective

Demonstrate a practical, repeatable workflow for using AI tools to support programme and proposal design for donors funding WASH, MCH/Nutrition, climate resilience, food security, and cross-sectoral innovation in the Horn of Africa.

Sample Training Module

Title: Using AI to Design Strong Concept Notes and Full Proposals

Target Group: Programme, MEAL, and business development staff in NGOs.

Duration: 3–4 hours (half‑day hands-on session).

Session Outline

  1. Identify a real or sample call for proposals (e.g., climate-resilient WASH for drought-affected communities).
  2. Use AI to summarize the call, eligibility, and evaluation criteria in plain language.
  3. Co-create a problem statement and theory of change using structured prompts.
  4. Draft a logframe (outputs, outcomes, indicators) with AI, then refine manually.
  5. Generate first-draft narrative sections (context, approach, innovation, risk) and improve them collaboratively.
  6. Develop a checklist to ensure compliance with donor requirements and ethical AI use.

Example AI Prompt Framework

Staff are trained to move from ad hoc prompting to a simple, structured prompt template:

  • Context: “You are a proposal designer for a humanitarian NGO working in the Horn of Africa…”
  • Inputs: Paste a short needs assessment, relevant SDG targets, and donor call text.
  • Task: “Draft a 2–3 paragraph problem statement focusing on WASH and climate resilience for pastoralist communities.”
  • Constraints: Word limit, donor language, gender and protection lenses, do‑no‑harm.
  • Review: Ask the AI to provide 3 options; staff compare, fact‑check, and localize wording.

Deliverables (Illustrative)

  • Step‑by‑step workflow guide (PDF/slide deck) for AI-supported proposal design.
  • Templates for AI prompts for problem statements, logframes, and narratives.
  • Checklist for combining AI outputs with human expertise, MEAL data, and community feedback.
