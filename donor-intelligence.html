AI-Supported Donor Intelligence & Landscape Analysis

Sample approach for using AI to scan donor priorities and funding opportunities.

Objective

Enable staff to use AI tools to synthesize large volumes of donor information, identify relevant opportunities, and maintain an up‑to‑date donor landscape for priority themes such as WASH, climate resilience, food security, and MCH/Nutrition.

Sample Donor Landscape Template

In training, participants populate a simple table and then use AI to summarize patterns.

  • Donor / Window name.
  • Thematic focus and geographic coverage.
  • Typical grant size and duration.
  • Target populations (e.g., displaced, pastoralist, women and youth).
  • Past relationship and alignment with organizational strategy.

AI is used to help generate short, comparable summaries of each donor window and highlight where climate resilience, peacebuilding, and social protection intersect.

Example Donor Intelligence Prompts

  • “Summarize the main priorities, geographic focus, and eligibility criteria of this call for proposals in no more than 200 words, focusing on opportunities for NGOs in the Horn of Africa.”
  • “Compare these three donor calls and list: key similarities, differences, and which one is the best fit for a resilience-focused WASH project in arid and semi-arid lands.”
  • “From the donor strategies provided, extract 5–7 keywords that should appear in our concept notes to reflect strong alignment.”

Deliverables (Illustrative)

  • Short guidance note on using AI for donor scanning and opportunity tracking.
  • Sample donor landscape sheet pre-populated for training exercises.
  • Prompt library for summarizing calls, flagging eligibility, and suggesting next steps.
